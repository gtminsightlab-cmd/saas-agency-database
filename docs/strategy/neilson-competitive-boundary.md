## What Neilson is

Neilson Marketing sells insurance-specific agent/broker/MGA/wholesaler data with state and vertical slicing. Pricing model: $10k entry (5+2 free states), $1,500/state add-on, $25k all-50 ceiling at renewal. Direct anchor for Agency Signal's Enterprise+ Distribution Expander pricing (D-015 50% undercut at the ceiling).

## What Neilson validates

Insurance organizations buy:
- agency data
- broker data
- producer data
- MGA / wholesaler data
- insurance company data
- segmentation
- data cleanse
- email / contact quality
- marketing-support data

## What Agency Signal should borrow

- state-based segmentation
- vertical segmentation
- verified contact emphasis
- data quality positioning
- enterprise distribution-growth framing
- insurance-specific data packaging (not generic B2B)

## What Agency Signal should avoid

- generic list-broker positioning
- "buy a list" commodity feel
- low-quality resale perception
- telemarketing-service framing
- broad undifferentiated data catalog
- "we sell emails" framing

## Our edge

| Neilson-style | Agency Signal-style |
|---|---|
| Data vendor | Intelligence layer |
| Lists | Dynamic agency universe |
| Campaign support | Distribution strategy |
| Broad insurance marketing | Commercial insurance segmentation |
| Static exports | Saved lists + refresh + change detection |
| Contact data | Agency × producer × appointment × vertical × territory intelligence |

## Pricing edge

- Neilson all-50: $25,000
- Agency Signal all-50 ceiling: **$12,500** (50% undercut per D-015)
- Plus: per-state pricing on **verified email contacts** (buyer's reachable-people KPI), not raw agency count. Tier 1 ≥5,000 emails @ $2,000/state (5 states: CA, MI, NY, OH, PA); Tier 2 2,000–4,999 @ $1,500/state (15 states); Tier 3 <2,000 @ $1,000/state (28 states); $0 territorial add-ons for <50 emails (AK, DC, HI).
- Plus: Distribution+ outcome SKU at $300–$500 per qualified appointment — Neilson's cost model precludes following this pricing motion.

## Suggested framing for sales / marketing

> Agency Signal helps insurance distribution teams identify, segment, and prioritize the agencies and producers most likely to fit their growth strategy.

NOT:

> Agency Signal is a cheaper Neilson alternative.

The undercut is a fact, not the wedge. The wedge is the intelligence layer.
